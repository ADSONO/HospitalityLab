# 2026-06-07

## Today

A perfectly normal Sunday for HospitalityLab.

Part of the day was spent improving the physical infrastructure of the satellite installation, while the rest was dedicated to beginning work on the web platform.

## Satellite Infrastructure

The satellite dish was moved closer to its intended long-term location.

As a result, the dish had to be aligned again and several adjustments were required to recover the Astra signal.

The satellite finder proved extremely useful during this process and made the realignment significantly easier than previous attempts.

Work also continued on cable routing.

A hole was drilled through the house wall with my father to allow the coaxial cables to pass into the garage in a cleaner and more permanent way.

At the moment, four coaxial cables have been installed for the quattro LNB.

However, additional cables will be required in the future for terrestrial television distribution and other planned infrastructure, meaning the opening will likely need to be enlarged later.

The coaxial cables were also routed through the garage as neatly as possible in preparation for future work.

## Web Platform

Started getting back into HTML and CSS for the public-facing part of the hotel, camping and restaurant website.

The objective is to begin developing the public side of the project while keeping the design simple and maintainable.

AI assistance is currently being used mainly for CSS, which is definitely not my favorite part of web development.

## Thoughts

Today was a mix of infrastructure work and software development.

The satellite installation is slowly moving from a temporary test setup towards something cleaner and more permanent.

At the same time, work has finally started on the web platform, which has been waiting in the background while most of the recent effort was focused on television, satellite and infrastructure systems.

HospitalityLab continues to grow one step at a time.
