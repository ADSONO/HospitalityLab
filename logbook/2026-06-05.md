2026-06-05

Today

Today was a very productive day for the satellite project.

Several important milestones were completed and, for the first time, the satellite installation became operational.

New Equipment

Received a second hand Vu+ Solo² satellite receiver.

The receiver will be used for testing, signal verification and future experiments within HospitalityLab.

Installation Work

Completed the installation of the F-type connectors on the coaxial cables.

Temporary cable runs were also installed in preparation for testing the satellite infrastructure.

Satellite Dish Alignment

A significant amount of time was spent aligning the satellite dish.

Without a dedicated satellite finder, the process proved to be much more difficult and time-consuming than expected.

After several hours of adjustments and testing, a usable satellite signal was finally obtained.

First Satellite Reception

Successfully received the first satellite channels.

At the moment, only a few foreign channels are available, but this confirms that:

* The dish is receiving signal
* The receiver is operational
* The basic satellite infrastructure is functioning

This marks the beginning of the satellite part of HospitalityLab.

Televes Equipment

The Televes equipment continues to be the most challenging part of the project.

Although some progress has been made, access to the system remains limited due to an unknown password.

Further investigation is required before the equipment can be fully evaluated and integrated.

Thoughts

Today felt like a major milestone.

After several days of preparation, cable work, hardware acquisition and dish alignment, the first satellite signal was finally received.

Sometimes the hardest part of a project is simply proving that everything works together.

Today was one of those days.
